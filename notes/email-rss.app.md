**Subject:** Developer API Partnership Request — Xentara Intelligence Platform

---

Dear RSS.app Team,

My name is Franco Benedetti, and I'm the founder of **Xentara** — a content intelligence and curation platform currently in active development. I'm reaching out to explore a no-cost developer API arrangement while we build toward a commercial launch.

**What Xentara Is**

Xentara is a hub-centric intelligence platform that helps curators — journalists, researchers, community leaders, and media organisations — monitor, analyse, and selectively distribute content from multiple sources to their audiences. Unlike a traditional RSS reader, Xentara runs an AI pipeline that summarises, classifies, and scores content against each curator's unique editorial "taxonomy," before pushing high-signal publications through Telegram channels, a consumer PWA, and a REST API for third-party integration.

Think of it as the layer between raw content sources and an engaged, curated audience.

**Where RSS.app Fits**

Our platform currently supports RSS and YouTube as source types. One of the most common frustrations our early curators encounter is adding sources from websites that have no native RSS feed, or feeds that are blocked for server-side fetching from cloud infrastructure — a problem particularly prevalent with regional news sites, civil-society organisations, and niche publications.

RSS.app's ability to generate a reliable, maintained feed URL from any public website is precisely the missing piece for our source onboarding flow. A curator would add a URL, Xentara would silently resolve it through the RSS.app API, and the resulting feed integrates seamlessly into our existing discovery pipeline — entirely invisible to the end user.

**The Mutual Benefit**

Xentara is designed from the ground up as a multi-tenant, white-label platform for hub operators. As we onboard paying curators and Hub operators, each new content source they add becomes a candidate for RSS.app resolution. At commercial scale, this represents a predictable, growing volume of feed generation requests — the kind of sustained, programmatic API usage that maps naturally onto your paid tier pricing.

We are not requesting free access in perpetuity. We are requesting **developer-tier access during our build and pilot phase** — roughly the next 6 months — to evaluate integration quality, estimate usage volume, and build RSS.app into our production architecture as a first-class dependency. A formal paid arrangement would follow at launch.

In addition, we are happy to:
- Credit RSS.app in our platform documentation and partner acknowledgements
- Provide early feedback on API behaviour across a diverse range of regional and non-English source sites
- Serve as a reference case for RSS.app's use in agentic/AI intelligence pipelines — an emerging use case your documentation does not yet prominently address

**Request**

If RSS.app offers a developer or partner programme, I would be grateful to be considered. If not, I would welcome a conversation about a trial arrangement under terms that work for your team.

You can learn more about the project at our repository or I am happy to schedule a brief call to walk you through the platform.

Thank you for considering this request. I look forward to hearing from you.

Warm regards,
**Franco Benedetti**
Founder, Xentara

A few notes on the approach:
- The email **leads with value, not need** — it frames RSS.app as a strategic dependency with ongoing commercial upside, not a free tool being borrowed
- It's **specific about the use case** — server-side WAF bypass and feedless sites — which demonstrates you actually understand what their product does
- The **"mutual benefit" section** gives them concrete reasons to say yes beyond goodwill
- It deliberately leaves **room for a negotiation** rather than demanding a specific tier
